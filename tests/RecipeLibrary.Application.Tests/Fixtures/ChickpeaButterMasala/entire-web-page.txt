CHICKPEA BUTTER MASALA (CHOLE BUTTER MASALA)
 


YOUTUBE LINK 🎥
Ingredients: Serves 5 to 6
For the Tomato Puree:
390 gm/ 3 medium tomatoes

11 gm/ 4 cloves garlic

15 gm/ 1-inch piece ginger

For the Curry Base:
1 tbsp oil

2 to 3 tbsp butter

185 gm/ 1 large onion, finely chopped

½ tbsp tomato paste (optional, for added richness)

½ tsp turmeric powder

1 heaped tsp Kashmiri chili powder

2 tsp ground coriander (dhania powder)

1 tsp ground cumin (jeera powder)

2 tsp + 1/4th salt (adjust to taste)

2 tsp sugar (to balance the flavors)

1 tsp garam masala powder

1 tsp dried fenugreek leaves (kasoori methi), crushed

¼ tsp black pepper powder

Other Ingredients:
1 large can (822 gm with liquid and weighs 484 gm after draining liquid) chickpeas, drained and rinsed (save ⅓ cup for thickening)

1 cup hot water + ½ cup used to puree the chickpeas

⅓ cup heavy cream (or coconut cream/milk for a dairy-free option)

2 tbsp chopped fresh coriander leaves

For the Final Garnish:
1 tbsp butter

1 drop oil

1 tsp sweet paprika (or Kashmiri chili powder)

Additional fresh coriander leaves and cream for drizzling

Instructions:
Step 1: Prepare the Tomato Puree
Blend the tomatoes, garlic, and ginger into a smooth puree. Set aside.

Step 2: Cook the Base
In a pot, heat oil and butter over medium heat.

Add finely chopped onions and ¼ tsp salt. Sauté until they turn light brown (not too dark).

Stir in the tomato paste and fry on medium-low heat for a minute.

Step 3: Spice It Up
Add turmeric, Kashmiri chili powder, coriander, and cumin powder.

Fry the spices on low heat for a few seconds, then add the blended tomato puree.

Cover and cook on medium heat for 5 minutes.

Stir, cover partially, and let it thicken.

Step 4: Add the Chickpeas
Stir in salt, sugar, garam masala, and crushed kasoori methi.

Add the drained chickpeas and mix well to coat them in the masala.

Pour in 1 cup hot water and let the flavors blend.

Step 5: Thicken the Gravy
Blend the reserved ⅓ cup chickpeas with ½ cup water to a smooth paste.

Add this puree to the curry to create a rich, thick consistency.

Stir in black pepper powder.

Cover and let it simmer on low heat for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally.

Step 6: Final Touches
Do a taste test and adjust the seasonings if needed.

Add chopped coriander leaves and ⅓ cup heavy cream (or coconut cream for a dairy-free option).

Cover and cook on low heat for another 5 minutes.

Step 7: The Secret Restaurant-Style Finish
In a small pan, heat butter with just a drop of oil over low heat.

Once melted, turn off the heat and stir in sweet paprika (or Kashmiri chili powder).

Drizzle this aromatic butter over the chickpea curry.

Serving Suggestions:
Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice for an irresistible meal! You can freeze this curry for 2 months. If you freeze it, follow step 7 just before serving.

QUICK & EASY VEGETABLE MASALA RICE (Fragrant Indian Spiced Rice)


YOUTUBE VIDEO LINK


This is an easy vegetable masala rice that takes just a few minutes to put together. It is a quick, flavorful, and colorful rice dish made with cooked rice, mixed vegetables, whole spices, and simple Indian masala powders.

Ingredients - Serves 5 to 6
690 gm / 5 ½  cups cooked rice, preferably cold

4  tablespoons + 1 tsp oil

1/2 teaspoon brown mustard seeds(rai)

1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds,(jeera)

75 gm / 1 medium  to large onion, finely chopped

10 gm / 3 to 4 garlic cloves, coarsely chopped

1 small broccoli cut into florets, cut small
You can use cauliflower instead, or leave it out.

1/2 red bell pepper, chopped

1/2 orange bell pepper, chopped

1 fresh green/red chili, cut into half or left whole

1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder

1/2 teaspoon Kashmiri chili powder, or any chili powder of your choice (optional)

2 teaspoons ground coriander,(dhania powder)

1 teaspoon ground cumin, (jeera powder)

1/2 cup frozen mixed vegetables

1 teaspoon garam masala or shahi garam masala

Salt, to taste. I used 2 teaspoons 

1 tsp sugar, optional

1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg (jaiphal)

2 tablespoons water

1 teaspoon ghee, optional

Chopped coriander leaves or any herb of your choice, to finish

A few drops of lemon/lime juice, to finish

Instructions
Use a large pan, wok, or pot for this recipe. A larger cooking surface helps the rice stay fluffy. If you use a small pan, the rice may become crowded and may not fry as well.

Heat oil over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, lower the heat and add the mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Let them crackle and splutter. This step is important because the whole spices release their flavor into the oil, creating a beautifully aromatic base for the rice.

Be careful not to let the seeds turn black, as they can become bitter.

Add the finely chopped onions and fry over medium-high heat. You do not need to brown them deeply. Cook until the onions soften slightly and turn pale.

Add the coarsely chopped garlic. Do not chop the garlic too finely for this recipe. Stir the onion and garlic together over medium heat for about a minute.

Now add the broccoli florets. Make sure they are cut into small pieces so they blend well with the rice. Stir over high heat until the broccoli turns a vibrant green color.

Add the chopped bell peppers and the fresh chili, if using. Give everything a brief stir.

Lower the heat before adding the powdered spices. Add turmeric powder, Kashmiri chili powder, ground coriander, and ground cumin. Stir briefly so the vegetables are coated with the spices.

Add the frozen mixed vegetables. Frozen vegetables work very well in this recipe and make it quick and convenient. Stir everything together.

Add the garam masala, salt, and a little sugar if you like. The sugar is optional, but it helps balance the flavors. Cook the vegetables briefly, but do not let them become mushy. They should hold their shape until the end.

Now add the cooked rice. Cold rice from the refrigerator works best because it stays firm and does not break easily.  Drizzle a little oil over the rice and sprinkle it with a little salt so the rice itself is seasoned.

Stir the rice gently over medium-high heat. Use light hands so the grains do not break. The goal is to keep the rice fluffy and separate, similar to fried rice.

After stirring for a few minutes, add the ground nutmeg. Then add 2 tablespoons of water and immediately cover the pan. Keep the heat low and let the rice steam for 2 minutes. This little bit of steam helps the spices and seasonings infuse into the rice and brings the whole dish together.

After 2 minutes, remove the lid. Add a teaspoon of ghee if you like. This step is optional, but it gives the rice a special aroma and richness.

Finish with a generous amount of chopped coriander leaves and a few drops of lemon juice. The lemon juice brightens the dish and balances the spices beautifully.

Serve hot.

Serving Suggestions
Vegetable masala rice goes well with:

Raita

Raita recipes on my channel

https://youtu.be/K9qJQmOeohU

https://youtu.be/hLJzJsVqWsE
Plain yogurt 

Salad

Vegetable curry with thick gravy

Tofu curry

Stir-fried chicken

Pickle and papad

It is also delicious on its own as a quick lunch or light dinner.

Storage Tips
This rice stores well in the refrigerator for a few days. Keep it in an airtight container and warm it in the microwave before serving.

It is also a great lunchbox recipe because the rice stays flavorful and satisfying even after reheating.

Tips for Best Results
Use cold cooked rice whenever possible. Cold rice stays firm and helps the grains remain separate.

Use a large pan or wok so the rice has enough space to fry properly.

Do not overcook the vegetables. They should stay colorful and hold their shape.

Add salt in stages. Seasoning each element lightly makes the final dish taste balanced without needing too much salt.

Let the rice steam for 2 minutes at the end. This helps all the spices and flavors come together.

EASY BUTTERMILK BAKED CHICKEN




YOUTUBE VIDEO LINK

I make this almost every week. It is one of our family favorites, and honestly, it always surprises me how something so easy can turn out so delicious. This buttermilk marinated baked chicken is soft, juicy, flavorful, and almost completely hands-off once it goes into the oven.

Ingredients - Serves 5 to 6

For the first marinade
1580 gm /3.5 lb Chicken thighs and/or drumsticks, bone in ,skin on

2 teaspoons salt

1 cup (standard measuring cup) buttermilk

Buttermilk substitute
3/4 cup plain unflavored whole milk yogurt

1/4 cup water

Mix the yogurt and water together until smooth and use it in place of buttermilk.

For the second marinade
1/4 cup oil

24 gm / 1 heaped tablespoon finely grated garlic

15 gm / 2 teaspoons finely grated ginger

1 tablespoon garlic powder

2 teaspoons ground cumin

1 tablespoon dried herb mix, such as Italian seasoning

1/4 teaspoon black pepper powder

1 teaspoon Kashmiri chili powder, or any chili powder of your choice

1 tablespoon smoked paprika, or regular paprika (optional)

Salt, to taste 

2 teaspoons brown sugar, or regular sugar (optional)

For the tray
1 large potato, sliced thinly

1 large onion, thickly sliced

1 teaspoon oil

A pinch of salt

A pinch of black pepper

For garnish
Chopped parsley

Chopped green onions

Instructions
Add the chicken pieces to a large bowl. Sprinkle with 2 teaspoons of salt, then pour in the buttermilk. Mix well so the chicken is coated on all sides. Cover and refrigerate for 8 to 10 hours, or overnight.

Take the chicken out of the refrigerator at least 30 minutes to 1 hour before cooking. This resting time is useful because we will use it for the second marinade.

In a bowl, add the oil, grated garlic, grated ginger, garlic powder, ground cumin, dried herbs, black pepper, Kashmiri chili powder, smoked paprika, salt, and brown sugar. Mix everything together well.

Remove the chicken pieces from the buttermilk marinade one by one and add them to the second marinade. Discard the leftover buttermilk marinade. It has done its job, so even though it may feel wasteful, do not reuse it.

Coat the chicken very well in the second marinade and allow it to sit for 30 minutes to 1 hour.

Preheat the oven to 375°F / 190°C if using a convection oven, or 400°F / 200°C if using a regular oven.

Take an oven-safe dish, skillet, or baking tray. Add the sliced potatoes and onions. Drizzle with 1 teaspoon of oil and season with a pinch of salt and black pepper. Mix well.

Place the marinated chicken pieces on top in a single layer. Do not waste any of that flavorful second marinade; spoon every bit of it over the chicken.

Bake for about 1 hour and 15 minutes. Oven temperatures can vary, so check the chicken occasionally as it bakes. Once you see liquid collecting at the bottom of the dish, carefully spoon that liquid over each piece of chicken to baste it. This may happen around 30 minutes before the chicken is done.

With a meat thermometer, check the internal temperature of the chicken before taking it out of the oven. It should reach at least 175°F and above for doneness.

Baste all the chicken pieces once more with the pan juices. Sprinkle with chopped parsley and green onions.

Serve hot with a fresh salad, rice, or bread.

Notes
This recipe is more of a flexible guide than a strict formula. You can change the seasonings based on what you have at home. Poultry seasoning, roasted garlic and herb seasoning, taco seasoning, kasoori methi, garam masala, or any favorite spice blend will work beautifully.

You can also skip the potatoes and use bell peppers instead. Just make sure the potatoes, if using, are sliced thinly so they cook through properly.

Apart from the long marination time, this recipe requires very little effort. At the end, the oven does most of the work and you get a gorgeous, juicy, flavorful chicken dinner that feels comforting and special.

QUICK & EASY GARLIC CHICKEN
                                          

YOUTUBE VIDEO LINK
Ingredients - Serves 5 to 6
For the Chicken Marinade
750 gm Boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-size pieces

1/2 tablespoon sweet paprika, or 1 tsp Kashmiri chili powder

1/4 teaspoon freshly crushed black pepper

1 teaspoon finely grated ginger

17 gm 1 heaped teaspoon finely grated garlic

1 tablespoon soy sauce

1/2 tablespoon vinegar

For Cooking
4 tbsp oil (adjust according to your preference)

20 gm coarsely chopped garlic (avoid cutting too fine as they may burn when frying)

128 gm Chopped onion

1 dry red chilli

1 tablespoon soy sauce

2 teaspoons vinegar

1 teaspoon Kashmiri chili powder 

Salt, to taste

60 gm Onion, cut into chunky pieces

120 gm Green bell pepper, cut into chunky pieces

Splash of water, if needed. I used about 1/4th cup of hot water.

2 to 3 whole green chillies (optional)

1/4 teaspoon crushed black pepper

A little less than 1 tablespoon tomato ketchup

Chopped coriander leaves or cilantro

Green onion greens, chopped

Instructions
Place the chicken in a bowl. Add sweet paprika or Kashmiri chili powder, crushed black pepper, grated ginger, grated garlic, soy sauce, and vinegar. Mix very well until the chicken is evenly coated.

Cover and marinate the chicken for a minimum of 15 minutes to 1 hour. 

Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Before the oil gets too hot, while it is still just warm, add the coarsely chopped garlic.

Fry the garlic over very low heat so it slowly infuses the oil. Cook until the edges of the garlic just start turning light brown.

Add the chopped onion and fry over medium-high heat until the onion turns pale.

I added a dry red chilli at this point but add it only if you want this dish to be spicy

Add the marinated chicken. Fry over high heat for 3 to 4 minutes.

Reduce the heat to medium. Add soy sauce, vinegar, and Kashmiri chili powder. Fry for 2 to 3 minutes.

Lower the heat, cover the pan, and cook for 15 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender.

Remove the lid and taste. Adjust seasoning if needed, adding salt to taste.

Add the chunky onion pieces and green bell pepper. Fry over medium-high heat for about 1 minute.

Add a splash of water if the mixture looks too dry.

Add crushed black pepper and tomato ketchup. Stir well.

Finish with chopped coriander leaves or cilantro and chopped green onion greens. Give everything a final stir and remove from heat.

Serve hot with fried rice, plain rice, or noodles.

Notes
Boneless skinless chicken thighs work best because they stay juicy and tender, but chicken breast can also be used.

Add the garlic while the oil is only slightly warm so it fries gradually and flavors the oil without burning.

Sweet paprika gives color without heat. Kashmiri chili powder can be used for a deeper red color and mild heat.

The tomato ketchup adds a subtle sweetness and tang that balances the soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and pepper.

BENGALI DALER BORAR JHOL (LENTIL FRITTER CURRY)
 


YOUTUBE VIDEO LINK
Ingredients - Serves 5 to 6
For the Lentil Fritters
148 gm / ¾ th cup chana dal, rinsed thoroughly and soaked overnight. I always use a little more as I have this tendency of snacking on some fried fritters when they come out hot from the pot.

1 teaspoon finely grated ginger

1 small green chilli, optional

1/4 cup water, or as needed for blending, but adding too much water will make the batter watery 

1/2 teaspoon salt

1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder

For the Curry
2 medium potatoes

2 ridge gourds, partially peeled and cut into thick pieces

 5 + 2 tablespoons oil 

1 to 2 dried red chillies (optional)

1 teaspoon nigella seeds, also called kalonji

1/4 teaspoon hing, also called asafoetida, optional

1 heaped teaspoon finely grated ginger

1 teaspoon ground cumin (jeera powder)

1 teaspoon turmeric powder (haldi)

1/2 teaspoon Kashmiri red chilli powder

Salt, to taste

1 teaspoon sugar or jaggery (optional)

1 - 2 tablespoons reserved lentil batter

1/2 cup water, for rinsing the blender jar

2 1/2 cups hot water, plus more if needed (I used a total of 3 & ¼ cups water)

Chopped coriander leaves, optional

Instructions
1. Soak and Blend the Lentils
Wash the chana dal well and soak it for 6 to 8 hours or overnight in plenty of water. The next day, discard the soaking water completely and rinse one more time. 

Transfer the soaked dal to a blender along with ginger, green chilli, and about 1/4 cup water. Blend to a thick paste. Add only as much water as needed to help the dal blend. The paste can be slightly coarse, but it should not be too grainy nor too runny.

2. Season and Whisk the Batter
Transfer the lentil paste to a bowl. Add salt and turmeric powder.

Whisk the batter very well for 2 to 3 minutes or until it turns light and fluffy. This step is important because whisking incorporates air into the batter, making the fritters lighter, softer, and fluffier after frying.

Reserve about 2 tablespoons of the batter for the gravy later.

3. Fry the Potatoes
Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Once hot, add the potato pieces.

Fry the potatoes on medium-high heat until they turn golden brown on the outside. Remove them from the pan and set aside.

4. Fry the Lentil Fritters
Whisk the lentil batter once more before frying.

Lower the heat before adding the batter to the oil. Drop small dollops of batter into the pan to make fritters. Beginners can use two teaspoons, spoon up the batter with a teaspoon and slide it into the oil with another teaspoon. These are called daler bora in Bengal where dal is lentil and bora are the fritters.

Let them cook undisturbed on medium-low heat for about a minute, or until they release naturally from the pan. Do not scrape them too early, or they may break.

Turn and fry until golden brown on all sides. Do not overbrown them. Remove and keep aside. Continue frying the remaining batter in batches, keeping the fritters in a single layer.

These fritters are delicious enough to eat on their own as a snack, so don’t be surprised if a few disappear before they reach the curry.

5. Start the Curry Base
If there is very little oil left in the pan, add about 2 tablespoons more.

Once the oil is hot, lower the heat to very low and add the dried red chillies, nigella seeds, and hing. Let them sizzle briefly until fragrant.

Add the ridge gourd pieces and fry on high heat until the edges turn lightly golden.

6. Add Potatoes and Spices
Add the grated ginger and stir well.

Add the fried potatoes, ground cumin, turmeric powder, Kashmiri red chilli powder, salt, and sugar or jaggery. Mix everything well so the vegetables are coated with the spices.

7. Add the Reserved Lentil Batter
Take the 2 tablespoons of reserved lentil batter and mix it with about 1/2 cup water. You can rinse the blender jar with this water to capture any remaining dal paste.

Pour this mixture over the vegetables. This  gives a wonderful lentil flavor to the gravy.

Add 2 1/2 cups hot water and stir well. The gravy should be on the thinner side because the lentil fritters will soak up a lot of liquid later.

8. Simmer the Vegetables
Cover and cook on medium-low heat for 6 to 7 minutes, or until the potatoes and ridge gourd are tender. 

Stir once or twice in between. If the gravy thickens too much, add a little more hot water.

Taste and adjust the salt, sweetness, or spices as needed.

9. Add the Lentil Fritters
Once the vegetables are cooked, add the fried lentil fritters to the gravy.

Simmer for only about 2 minutes on medium heat. Do not cook them for too long, or they may become too soft and start breaking apart.

Add a splash of hot water if the gravy looks too thick.

Finish with chopped coriander leaves if using.

Serving Suggestions
Serve this Bengali lentil fritter curry hot with steamed rice. It also pairs well with roti, but rice is the most traditional and comforting option.

Keep the gravy slightly thin when serving, as the fritters continue to absorb liquid even after cooking.

Tips for the Best Daler Bora Curry
Whisk the lentil batter very well before frying. This makes the fritters light and fluffy instead of dense.

Do not add too much water while blending the dal. The batter should be thick enough to hold its shape when dropped into hot oil.

Do not move the fritters immediately after adding them to the pan. Let them release naturally before turning.

If using zucchini, cut it into large chunks and add it later in the cooking process, when the potatoes are almost tender. Zucchini cooks quickly and can fall apart if added too early.

Keep the gravy thinner than you think you need. The lentil fritters absorb a lot of liquid.

Variations
You can replace ridge gourd with zucchini, bottle gourd, cauliflower, carrots, butternut squash, or other summer squash. I sometimes make this with only potatoes if there are no vegetables at home.

You can also use different lentils such as red lentils, mung dal, or yellow split peas, though chana dal gives the best nutty flavor and texture.

For a milder version, skip the green chilli and reduce or omit the dried red chillies.

CREAMY GREEN CURRY INDIAN STYLE (VEGAN)


YOUTUBE LINK

This Indian-style creamy green curry is packed with vegetables, white beans, tofu, and fresh herbs. It’s rich from coconut milk, full of flavor, and easy to adapt with whatever vegetables or protein you have on hand.

Ingredients - Serves 6 to 7
11 gm garlic 

20 gm ginger

180 gm / 1 large onion

¼ cup water, plus more as needed

4 to 5 tablespoons oil

1 teaspoon cumin seeds

½ teaspoon turmeric powder

1 teaspoon ground cumin (jeera powder)

1 teaspoon ground coriander (dhania powder)

1 red bell pepper, chopped

1 green bell pepper, chopped

1 15 oz (439 gm) can cooked white beans, drained and rinsed

6 to 7 sugar snap peas

1 can/ 400 ml coconut milk (save ⅓ rd cup for later)

½ cup hot water

Salt, to taste

50 gm cilantro (coriander leaves)

40 gm spinach ( only leaves)

15 gm fresh basil

1 green chili, optional

½ cup green beans, fresh or frozen

½ teaspoon ground black pepper

1 teaspoon garam masala

200 gm tofu cubes, or paneer

1 - 2 tsp lemon/lime juice

Instructions
Blend the garlic, ginger, onion, and ¼ cup water into a smooth paste.

Heat oil in a pan over medium-high heat. Reduce the heat to low and add cumin seeds. Let them sizzle, but do not let them burn.

Add the onion, garlic, and ginger paste. Cook over medium-low heat, stirring often.

Add turmeric, ground cumin, and ground coriander. Cook until the mixture becomes thick and pasty.

Add the bell peppers, white beans, and snap peas. Stir well.

Pour in most of the coconut milk, saving about ⅓ cup for blending the greens. Add ½ cup hot water and salt. Cover and simmer on low heat for 5 minutes.

Blend cilantro, spinach, basil, optional green chili, the reserved coconut milk, and ¼ cup water until smooth.

Add green beans and the green herb puree to the curry. Stir well.

Add black pepper, garam masala, and more salt if needed.

Add tofu or paneer and simmer uncovered for 5 minutes. 

Add lemon/lime juice and serve

Notes
Use chickpeas instead of white beans, paneer instead of tofu, or any vegetables you like. Fresh green beans should be added with the bell peppers, while frozen green beans can go in near the end. You can also add a teaspoon of sugar if desired.

Serving Suggestion
Serve with rice, roti, bread, or enjoy it on its own as a hearty meal.

BENGALI LIGHT CHICKEN JHOL
 


YOUTUBE VIDEO 
Ingredients - Serves 6 to 7
1 kg 500 gm Bone-in chicken pieces

4 tablespoons oil

1 dry bay leaf

4 green cardamoms

1 small cinnamon stick

1 heaped teaspoon panch phoron

1 large onion, half sliced and half for paste

1 large tomato

35 gm / 1 and 1/2 tablespoons grated ginger

1/3rd  cup chopped celery, optional

2 large Potatoes, cut into large pieces

2 Carrots, cut into large pieces

½ of a medium sized Green papaya, cut into large pieces

10 - 12 Green beans, fresh or frozen

1 teaspoon turmeric powder

1/2 teaspoon Kashmiri red chilli powder, optional

1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper (please adjust proportion according to your preference)

Salt, to taste

2 to 3 cups hot water. Adjust consistency according to your preference

Instructions
Blend half of the onion with the tomato to make a smooth puree.

Heat oil in a pressure cooker, Instant Pot, or heavy-bottomed pot.

Lower the heat and add bay leaf, cardamoms, and cinnamon stick. Stir briefly.

Add panch phoron and let the seeds sizzle until aromatic. Do not burn them.

Add the onion tomato puree and stir for a minute.

Add chopped celery and grated ginger. Stir again.

Add the chicken pieces and mix briefly.

Add turmeric powder, Kashmiri chilli powder, black pepper, and salt.

Add potatoes, carrots, green papaya, and other vegetables of your choice.

Add 2 to 3 cups of hot water.

If using a pressure cooker, cook on high till full pressure is reached , then lower the heat to low and cook for 15 minutes. Let the pressure release naturally.

If using an Instant Pot, pressure cook on high for 15 minutes and allow natural pressure release.

If using a regular pot, cover and simmer on low heat until the chicken and vegetables are cooked through.

Add frozen green beans at the end, if using, and cook for 1 minute.

Switch off the heat and serve hot.

Notes
Bone-in chicken is highly recommended because it gives the broth more flavor.

Garlic is optional. You can add a few cloves while blending the onion and tomato, but the recipe tastes lovely without it.

Keep the vegetables large so they do not break down completely and turn mushy.

Add more hot water as needed. This jhol should be light and brothy, not thick.

Kashmiri chilli powder is optional and can be skipped when making this for someone who is sick or sensitive to spice.

This Bengali-style light chicken jhol is warm, nourishing, and gentle. Whether you are making it for yourself or for someone you care about, it is pure comfort in a bowl.

TOFU MASALA CURRY RECIPE
 


YOUTUBE VIDEO
Ingredients - Serves 5 to 6
For frying the tofu
450 gm Firm / Extra-firm tofu

2 tablespoons oil

1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder

1/4 teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder

Sprinkle of salt when frying

For the masala gravy
2 tablespoons oil

2 tablespoons ghee

1 dry bay leaf

1 teaspoon cumin seeds

1 onion, finely chopped

Salt, to taste

25 gm ginger finely grated or ginger paste

20 gm garlic finely grated or garlic paste

2 tsp sugar (optional)

145 gm / 2 small to medium tomatoes, pureed

50 gm / 2 heaped tablespoons plain whole milk yogurt

2 tablespoons gram flour, also called besan

1 teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder

1 to 2 teaspoons sweet paprika

1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder

1 heaped teaspoon coriander powder (dhania powder)

1 teaspoon homemade garam masala, or 2 teaspoons store-bought garam masala

1/2 cup water, for the yogurt mixture

1 to 2 cups hot water, for the gravy

1 teaspoon crushed kasoori methi, dried fenugreek leaves

Chopped coriander leaves, for finishing

Instructions
1. Press the tofu
Drain the tofu and discard the water it was packed in. Rinse it briefly under tap water, then press it to remove as much excess moisture as possible.

A tofu press works well, but you can also wrap the tofu in paper towels and place a heavy object on top. Pressing helps the tofu fry better and gives it a firmer texture.

2. Fry the tofu
Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a nonstick pan over medium heat. When the oil is warm, not hot, add the turmeric powder and Kashmiri red chili powder.

Add the tofu pieces in a single layer. Sprinkle some salt over the tofu and let them cook undisturbed until lightly browned on one side, then turn them gently and brown the other side.

Do not force the tofu if it sticks to the pan. Give it another minute or two, and it should release more easily. Once lightly golden on both sides, remove the tofu and set it aside.

3. Start the gravy
In the same pan, add 2 tablespoons of oil and 2 tablespoons of ghee. Add the bay leaf and cumin seeds. Let the cumin seeds sizzle for a few seconds.

Add the chopped onion along with a pinch of salt. Cook over medium heat until the onions soften and begin to brown.

Add the grated ginger and garlic. Cook for about 1 minute over medium-low heat.

Add a little sugar and continue cooking until the onions look soft, jammy, and lightly caramelized.

4. Add the tomatoes
Pour in the pureed tomatoes. If needed, add a splash of water to the blender jar to rinse out the remaining tomato puree and add that to the pan too.

Cook the tomatoes over medium heat, stirring from time to time, until the mixture thickens and the oil starts to leave the sides of the pan.

5. Make the yogurt-spice mixture
In a bowl, add the yogurt and besan. Then add the Kashmiri red chili powder, sweet paprika, turmeric powder, coriander powder, and garam masala.

Pour in 1/2 cup water and whisk until smooth. A wire whisk helps remove lumps.

For best results, take the yogurt out of the refrigerator about 30 minutes before cooking. Room-temperature yogurt is less likely to split when added to the hot gravy.

6. Add the yogurt mixture carefully
Lower the heat to low. Add the yogurt-spice mixture to the pan (stir the yogurt spice mix well before adding to the pan) and immediately start stirring.

Keep stirring continuously until you see small bubbles appear. This step is important because it helps prevent the yogurt from splitting.

Once the yogurt is fully mixed in, continue cooking, stirring often, until the oil begins to surface again.

7. Simmer the gravy
Add 1 cup of hot water and stir well. If the gravy looks too thick, add another cup of hot water.

Add salt to taste. You can also add an extra teaspoon of sweet paprika at this stage if you want a deeper red color.

Stir in the crushed kasoori methi. Cover and simmer on low heat for about 5 minutes, stirring once or twice so nothing sticks to the bottom.

8. Add the tofu
Taste the gravy and adjust the salt or spices if needed. Add the fried tofu and mix gently so the pieces do not break.

Cover and cook on low heat for another 3 - 4 minutes.

Finish with chopped coriander leaves. Remove the bay leaf before serving.

Serving Suggestions
Serve masala tofu hot with steamed rice, jeera rice, roti, paratha, naan, or pulao. The gravy thickens as it cools because of the besan, so add a splash of hot water while reheating if needed.

Helpful Tips
Use extra-firm tofu for the best texture. Pressing the tofu is important because it removes excess water and helps the tofu brown nicely.

Keep the oil only warm when adding dry spice powders for frying the tofu. If the oil is too hot, the spices can burn quickly.

Do not rush the onions and tomatoes. Cooking them properly builds the base flavor of the curry.

When adding yogurt, keep the heat low and stir constantly until bubbles appear. This helps keep the gravy smooth and creamy.

Kasoori methi has a distinct flavor and there is no perfect substitute. The curry will still taste good without it, but it adds a lovely finishing aroma.

SKILLET POTATOES WITH DILL (ALOO SOYA SAAG BHAJI)





YOUTUBE VIDEO


This easy and flavorful potato stir fry is made with fresh dill leaves. It’s simple, comforting, and comes together quickly. You can serve it with rice or roti along with dal, vegetable curry, or even fish or meat. It also packs well for lunch and tastes great at room temperature.
Ingredients - Serves 3 to 4

480 gm / 4 medium potatoes (red or any variety), chopped (with or without skin)

65 gm fresh dill leaves, washed and roughly chopped (thick stems discarded)

2–3 tablespoons oil

½ teaspoon black mustard seeds

½ teaspoon cumin seeds

1 teaspoon turmeric powder

½ teaspoon Kashmiri chili powder (or any chili powder)

1 medium onion, sliced

2 - 3 large cloves garlic, chopped

2 whole green chilies (optional)

Salt to taste

Instructions

Heat oil in a pan over medium-high heat. Once hot, reduce the heat to low.

Add mustard seeds and let them crackle. Then add cumin seeds.

Add the chopped potatoes and spread them evenly in the pan.

Make a small space in the pan and add turmeric and chili powder. Mix well so the potatoes are evenly coated.

Add sliced onions and chopped garlic. Stir on medium heat for 2–3 minutes.

Lower the heat, cover, and cook for 2–3 minutes. Stir occasionally and cover again.

Add whole green chilies if using. Continue cooking on low heat until the potatoes are tender.

Once tender, increase the heat to medium-high, add salt, and the chopped dill leaves.

Stir fry for a few minutes without covering the pan, allowing the potatoes to crisp slightly and the dill to lightly fry.

Taste and adjust salt if needed. Serve hot or at room temperature.

Serving Suggestions
Serve with steamed rice or roti along with dal and a fresh salad. It also works well as a side dish for any Indian meal.

Notes & Tips

Keeping the potato skin on adds a nice texture in stir fry dishes, but you can peel them if you prefer.

Don’t cover the pan after adding dill. This keeps the flavor fresh and slightly roasted.

This recipe pairs perfectly with a simple dal and rice for an easy meal.

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. Reheat before eating.

MY ULTIMATE BUTTER CHICKEN RECIPE (MURGH MAKHANI)



Butter chicken is one of those dishes that shows up on almost every Indian restaurant menu, and for good reason. It’s rich, comforting, and full of flavor. The good news is you can absolutely make a version at home that tastes just as good, if not better.This recipe uses a double marinade for juicy, flavorful chicken and a silky smooth tomato-based sauce that brings everything together.
Ingredients - Serves 5 to 6
For the Chicken Marinade
First Marinade

1 kg boneless, skinless chicken thighs (cut into large pieces)

1 tsp salt

1 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder

1 tbsp lemon juice

Second Marinade

2 tbsp oil

1 tsp Kashmiri chili powder

½ tsp turmeric powder

1 tsp garam masala powder

½ cup plain unflavored thick Greek whole milk yogurt

1 tsp kasoori methi (dried fenugreek leaves)

¼ tsp black pepper powder

1 tsp ginger paste

1 tsp garlic paste

1 tsp salt

For the Sauce
1 tbsp oil

8 green cardamom pods (choti elaichi)

550 gm of canned San Marzano tomatoes (or any canned tomatoes or fresh ripe tomatoes)

½ cup water

2 tsp finely grated ginger

2 tsp finely grated garlic

Salt to taste

For the Final Butter Chicken
⅓ rd cup Butter (adjust according to your preference)

⅓ cup water

1 tbsp Kashmiri chili powder

1 tsp sweet paprika

2 tsp kasoori methi (dried fenugreek leaves)

1 tsp garam masala (or 2 tsp if store-bought)

½ tsp ground mace (or nutmeg)

2 whole green chilies (optional)

1 tbsp Honey ( or to to taste)/ use sugar

¼ cup heavy cream (+ extra for garnish)

Fresh coriander leaves

Instructions
Step 1: First Marinade
In a bowl, combine chicken with salt, chili powder, and lemon juice.
Let it sit for 45 minutes to 1 hour.

This step draws out moisture, which helps the second marinade coat the chicken better.

Step 2: Second Marinade
In a separate bowl, mix oil, spices, yogurt, kasoori methi, pepper, ginger, garlic and salt.

Discard any liquid released from the first marinade, then coat the chicken in this mixture.

Marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours, ideally overnight.

Step 3: Bake the Chicken
Take out the chicken from the refrigerator at least 1 hour before cooking

Preheat oven to 400°F/200 C (convection) or 425°F/218 C (regular)

Place chicken on a rack over a lined baking tray

Brush with melted butter

Bake for 40 minutes, flipping halfway and brushing with more butter.

Once done, cut into bite-sized pieces.

Step 4: Make the Sauce
Heat oil and add cardamom pods

Add tomatoes, water, ginger, garlic, and salt

Simmer on medium-low until thickened

Blend until smooth, then strain for a velvety texture (optional but recommended).

Step 5: Build the Flavor Base
Mix:

⅓ cup water

Kashmiri chili powder

Sweet paprika

Adjust spice levels to your preference.

Step 6: Finish the Butter Chicken
Melt butter in a pan

Add chili water mixture (careful, it may splatter)

Add the blended sauce and simmer for 15 minutes, stirring it occasionally 

Adjust salt as needed.

Then add:

Crushed Kasoori methi

Garam masala powder

Ground Mace or nutmeg

Baked chicken

Optional green chilies

Simmer covered for 5 minutes.

Step 7: Final Touches
Add honey to balance acidity

Stir in heavy cream

Simmer uncovered for 2 minutes

Garnish with coriander and extra cream

Tips for Best Results
Chicken thighs give the best flavor and stay juicy. You can also use chicken with bones.

Don’t skip the double marinade

If you have an outdoor charcoal grill, please grill the chicken instead of baking and enjoy the restaurant flavor to the fullest.

            https://youtu.be/MalEH8DsKrs


Straining the sauce gives that restaurant-style finish

Adjust spice levels to your taste, especially chili powder

Make Ahead Option
You can prepare the sauce in advance. When ready to serve:

Bake the chicken

Reheat the sauce

Combine and finish with cream

You can freeze this happily

PALAK PANEER (INDIAN COTTAGE CHEESE IN SPINACH GRAVY)




Palak Paneer is a very popular dish both at Indian restaurants and also in common households. Palak in Hindi is spinach and paneer is Indian cottage cheese.There are so many ways to make palak paneer. I’ve tried adding yogurt, tomatoes, frying the paneer, even finishing with lemon juice. But after all that experimenting, I’ve come to one conclusion: this dish tastes best when kept simple.
Ingredients - Serves 3 to 4
For the spinach puree

200 m fresh spinach (only leaves)

1/3 cup chopped coriander leaves (cilantro)

1 green chili (optional)

1/2 cup cold chilled water

For the curry

360 g paneer, cubed

1 tbsp oil + 1 tbsp heaped ghee 

1 tsp cumin seeds (jeera)

3 to 4 large garlic cloves, coarsely crushed

250 gm / 1 medium to large onion, finely chopped

Pinch of salt (for onions)

1/4 tsp turmeric powder

1/4 - ½ cup water (to rinse blender)

1/4 tsp kasoori methi (dried fenugreek leaves)

1/2 tsp garam masala powder

Salt to taste. I used 1 & ½ tsp

1 tsp sugar (optional)

1/4 cup heavy cream (or milk for a lighter version)

Instructions
1. Blanch the spinach
Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the spinach leaves and blanch for exactly 1 minute. Do not cover the pot, as that can dull the color.

Immediately transfer the spinach to a bowl of chilled water (you can add ice cubes). Let it sit for a few seconds, then squeeze out all the water.

2. Make the green paste
Add the blanched spinach to a blender along with coriander leaves, green chili, and cold water.

Blend until smooth. Using cold water helps retain that vibrant green color.

3. Start the base
Heat oil and ghee in a pan over medium heat.

Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add crushed garlic and sauté briefly. Then add chopped onions and a pinch of salt.

Cook until the onions soften and turn pale. Do not brown them, as it will affect the color of the gravy.

4. Build the curry
Add turmeric powder and pour in the spinach puree.

Rinse the blender with water and add it to the pan. Mix well.

Add kasoori methi, garam masala, and salt to taste. I also add a teaspoon of sugar that sometimes helps with the bitterness of spinach. Let it cook on medium heat for a few minutes.

5. Add paneer
Add paneer cubes and gently mix.

Do not fry the paneer beforehand. Frying makes it chewy and rubbery. You want it soft and delicate.

Cook for just 2–3 minutes. Overcooking can dull the color and make the spinach taste slightly bitter.

6. Finish with cream
Add cream (or milk if you prefer a lighter version). Mix and do a final taste check. Adjust salt if needed. Drizzle some cream over top before serving.

Serving Suggestions
Serve hot with roti, naan, or steamed rice. Add a drizzle of cream on top if you like.
